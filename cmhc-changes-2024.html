📰 CMHC Changes 2024: What Canadian Homebuyers Need to Know

Understanding the latest updates to mortgage insurance rules can significantly impact your home-buying journey.

The Canada Mortgage and Housing Corporation (CMHC) periodically updates its mortgage insurance rules, which can affect how much you need for a down payment, your eligibility for a mortgage, and the overall cost of your home. For 2024, several key areas have seen adjustments designed to promote housing stability and affordability.

Key Updates for 2024

While specific changes can vary, common areas of focus for CMHC updates include:

  • Down Payment Requirements: No major changes to minimum down payments (5% for homes under $500K, 10% on the portion between $500K-$1M) are expected, but ongoing discussions about higher-value homes continue.
  • Debt Service Ratios (GDS/TDS): The Gross Debt Service (GDS) and Total Debt Service (TDS) ratios remain critical. CMHC continues to monitor these to ensure borrowers can manage their mortgage and other debts.
  • Stress Test Rate: The qualifying rate (stress test) continues to be the greater of your contract rate plus 2%, or 5.25%. This is a crucial factor in determining your maximum mortgage amount.
  • Energy-Efficient Homes: CMHC often offers premium refunds for energy-efficient homes. New criteria or enhanced incentives might be introduced to encourage green building.
  • Self-Employed Borrowers: There may be ongoing refinements to how income is assessed for self-employed individuals, aiming to make it easier for them to qualify while maintaining prudent lending standards.

How These Changes Affect You

These updates can influence your borrowing capacity, the amount of mortgage insurance you pay, and even the types of homes you might consider. It's always recommended to speak with a mortgage professional to understand the full impact on your specific situation.
